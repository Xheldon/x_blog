Mostly Harmless

当我跨过沉沦的一切，向着永恒开战的时候，你是我的军旗

The elements of this site are from the novel series The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy.

A Sagittarius man born in '92, I have many peculiar ideas that don’t quite match my current age. Whether online or offline, I’m not great at debating with strangers (almost no one admits being wrong to strangers online) but enjoy “rambling nonsense” with acquaintances.

My main profession is as a frontend engineer. Since quitting my accounting job in Foshan in September 2015 and moving to Beijing to work in frontend development, I’ve been in the internet industry for 7 years (as of 2022).

Of course, my journey in frontend—and even computer science—learning didn’t begin in 2015. During university, I often audited classes at the School of Software, so I have a basic grasp of foundational computer science courses like algorithms and data structures.

I love the internet and coding, so during my four years at university, I studied frontend technologies like HTML, CSS, Photoshop, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, and other essential skills for a “page-maker.” I paid particular attention to JavaScript and the then-nascent NodeJS.

In school, I experimented with Grunt, NodeJS, and AngularJS. I realized that to deeply understand a language, one must know its good and bad parts to compare it with others. To enhance my understanding, I also spent my spare time learning the syntax and operational principles of C, VB, C#, Java, and Android—all without neglecting my major courses.

During university, I joined the campus web club and the school CERT club. When the seniors there learned I wasn’t a computer science major, they kindly helped me grasp many fundamental concepts and common knowledge in the field, greatly aiding my self-study journey. I even won a team third prize in the school’s software design competition as a frontend developer. For this, I’m deeply grateful.

Through continuous learning, I’ve discovered my fascination with the deeper principles of computing—memory allocation, binary systems, network communication protocols, compiler theory, CPU principles, database fundamentals, data transmission, and other low-level computer basics. To fill these gaps, I’m currently supplementing my computer science knowledge using the University Computer Science course system on NetEase Cloud Classroom and related books.

Though my work revolves around web applications, I’m very interested in client-side development and dream of someday creating desktop/mobile apps used by millions. As such, I’m also intrigued by frameworks like Electron, React Native, and Flutter.

My current focus is on JavaScript/TypeScript. I enjoy using technology to solve everyday problems, such as writing Chrome extensions and AppleScript scripts to automate tasks. I advocate for high-efficiency work and aim to become a full-stack engineer (with a tooling focus).

Though I’m nearing 30, with over five years left until 35, I still love wild, chaotic imagination—something my wife often teases me about. Later, under the pressure of buying a home, my flights of fancy have somewhat reined in.
